# CyberScool Mission

CyberScool builds a secure hybrid research, development, automation, networking, deployment, and agent-collaboration platform that completes verified work across local devices, mobile clients, cloud services, virtual private servers, and private servers.

The platform exists to turn an owner-approved objective into a traceable result. It coordinates people, specialist agents, tools, and runtimes while keeping authority with the user. Work is useful only when it is implemented, tested, validated, reviewed, evidenced, and approved at the level its risk requires.

## Mission principles

- Owner control: users define scope, grant authority, approve sensitive transitions, and can stop work.
- Secure by default: untrusted work begins in a sandbox with minimum privileges and deny-by-default access.
- Evidence over claims: status is derived from durable events and artifacts, never agent confidence alone.
- Portable execution: one control plane can target local, Docker, VPS, cloud, and private-server runtimes through explicit capabilities.
- Specialist collaboration: the Copilot Manager delegates bounded work and keeps ownership, handoffs, and review visible.
- Isolation: organization and workspace boundaries apply to identity, memory, secrets, tools, artifacts, and audit records.
- Progressive delivery: feature flags and staged approvals limit blast radius.

## Definition of success

CyberScool succeeds when an authorized user can submit a task, understand its plan and risk, approve the required actions, follow specialist handoffs, inspect evidence, and receive a verified result without surrendering control of credentials, infrastructure, or completion decisions.
